What Do These Numbers Mean?

The questions you answered and tasks you performed measured your strengths in two vital areas: Neuroperformance, or how your brain works and Lifestyle, those activities in your everyday life that support brain health.

We combined these results to give you one simple score. The graphs below indicate how you scored relative to others in your age group. Because science tells us that some cognitive functions decline as we age, while others remain stable, comparing your score to others of the same age is a valuable gauge.

You Should Use These Numbers To:

  • Gain insight into strengths and target areas where you can improve
  • Map out how to improve, prioritizing the recommended activities
  • Build brain-healthy habits into your life, over time, to improve your score

My Future Brain

The trajectory below depicts how your current neuro-performance score may play out over your lifetime, assuming your score remains static. While science shows that it is normal for our cognitive abilities to decline over time, scientists also have good news. A holistic approach to a brain-healthy lifestyle can actually enhance brain performance as you age. By making simple changes, you can make a tangible difference in how independently and actively you continue to live, work and play.

The trajectory does not take into consideration factors such as family history, current overall health, how you are managing any chronic conditions you may have, or other factors that may impact your cognitive performance. It is determined relative to others that match your age, sex, and level of education.
